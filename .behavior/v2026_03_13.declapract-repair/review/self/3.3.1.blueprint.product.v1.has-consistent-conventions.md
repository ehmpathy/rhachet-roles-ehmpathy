## additional scrutiny: dot notation vs hyphen notation

### question: should stone names use dots or hyphens?

examined extant behavior route:
- `3.3.1.blueprint.product.v1.stone` — uses dots between semantic parts
- `v2026_03_13.declapract-repair` — uses hyphen in route slug

proposed stones:

- `3.1.repair.test.defects.stone` — uses dots

**analysis**: dots separate hierarchical parts (phase.name.type). hyphens join compound words within a part. blueprint follows this convention correctly.

**verdict**: **holds**.

### question: should `defects` be singular or plural?

examined extant terminology:
- behavior route uses plural: `3.1.repair.test.defects`
- domain objects typically use singular: `Task`, `Invoice`
- but collections use plural: `getAllTasks`, `defects` (the collection)

**analysis**: the stone repairs multiple defects, not a single defect. plural is appropriate for the action scope.

**verdict**: **holds**.

### question: is `cicd` the right term or should it be `ci` or `pipeline`?

examined extant usage:
- `git release --watch` — watches cicd pipeline
- behavior brief uses "cicd" terminology
- wish uses "cicd"

**alternatives considered**: `ci`, `pipeline`, `workflow`

**analysis**: `cicd` is precise — it covers both continuous integration and continuous delivery. `ci` would be incomplete. `pipeline` is generic. `workflow` could mean many things.

**verdict**: `cicd` is appropriate. **holds**.

---

### question: why `repair` instead of `fix`?

**analysis**:
- `repair` implies restoration to working state (domain-appropriate)
- `fix` is informal and could imply a hack
- `repair` pairs naturally with `defects` (we repair defects)
- extant vocabulary check: no strong preference in codebase

**verdict**: `repair` is appropriate and pairs well with `defects`. **holds**.

---

### question: why `reflect` instead of `review`?

examined behavior route vocabulary:
- behavior routes use "reflect" for introspection phases
- "review" could be confused with code review
- "reflect" implies classification and feedback, not approval

**verdict**: `reflect` is domain-appropriate and matches behavior route vocabulary. **holds**.
