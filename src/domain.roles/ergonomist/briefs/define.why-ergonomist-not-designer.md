# why ergonomist, not designer

## .what

we use "ergonomist" instead of "designer" for this role.

## .why

### etymology reveals intent

**ergonomics** = Greek *ergon* (work) + *nomos* (natural laws)
- literally: "the science of work"
- coined 1857 by Wojciech Jastrzebowski
- adopted formally 1949-1950 by British Admiralty

the term carries inherent purpose: **optimize human well-being and system performance**.

### designer is overloaded

"designer" suffers semantic diffusion:
- graphic designer (visual aesthetics)
- fashion designer (clothing)
- interior designer (spaces)
- game designer (mechanics)
- product designer (features)
- UX designer (interfaces)
- system designer (architecture)

when everything is design, the term signals no specific intent.

### ergonomist is fundamental

ergonomics predates and underlies all design disciplines:
- physical ergonomics: body mechanics, posture, fatigue
- cognitive ergonomics: perception, memory, reasoning, workload
- organizational ergonomics: systems, policies, processes

every good "design" is actually applied ergonomics — fitting the work to the human, not the human to the work.

### three domains of ergonomics

| domain | concerns |
|--------|----------|
| physical | anthropometry, biomechanics, posture, repetition |
| cognitive | perception, memory, decision-making, mental load |
| organizational | sociotechnical systems, teamwork, communication |

software systems engage all three domains — the ergonomist considers each.

### specificity enables clarity

| term | implies | ambiguity |
|------|---------|-----------|
| designer | make it pretty? | high |
| ergonomist | fit work to human | low |

"ergonomist" immediately communicates: we optimize for human capability and limitation.

### applies universally

ergonomics extends beyond interfaces:
- cli: command structure, output readability
- web: layout, navigation, feedback loops
- app: gestures, transitions, accessibility
- api: endpoint names, error messages
- sdk: method signatures, documentation
- comms: email clarity, notification cadence
- lifecycle: upgrade paths, migration experiences

anywhere humans interact with systems, ergonomics applies.

## .summary

"ergonomist" reminds us why the role exists: not to decorate, but to fit work to humans. the term is more specific, less ambiguous, and more fundamental than "designer".
